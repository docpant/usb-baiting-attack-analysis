# Attacker Mindset Analysis

An attacker could use the information stored on the USB drive to conduct phishing attacks, impersonation attempts, or social engineering campaigns targeting Jorge Bailey, hospital employees, or family members.

Sensitive work-related files could provide insight into hospital operations, employee schedules, and internal business processes. If the USB drive had been intentionally planted as part of a USB baiting attack, plugging it into a company workstation could have allowed malware installation or unauthorized network access.
